Description

Gifts don't have to be big and expensive: it's the thought that counts. However, it's a good idea to chip in for a really nice gift with a group of people. For example, it could be a good tradition to chip in with some family members to get gifts for Mother's and Father's day. In this stage, you will make it possible to split a certain sum between people in predefined groups.

We will think of the lists of names as groups of people. You should implement some basic operations on these groups: create and show to print out the people that the group consists of. Note: group names must be UPPERCASE to distinguish them from persons.

If you really think of it, splitting the price of a gift can be thought of as people lending and borrowing money among themselves. For example, Ann and Bob went to a cafe and spent 10 bucks there:
Ann: +5 (coffee) -5, owes to the future payer;
Bob: +5 (coffee) -5, owes to the future payer.

Then, Bob paid 10.00 dollars with a credit card:
Ann +5 (coffee) -5 owes Bob;
Bob +5 (coffee) -10 (paid bill) +5 (expecting a repay from Ann).

The transaction above will look something like this: purchase Bob coffee 10.00 (Bob, Ann)

Ann repaid Bob 5 dollars to cover her part of the bill:
Ann +5 (coffee) -5 (repaid Bob);
Bob +5 (coffee) -10 (paid the bill) +5 (from Ann).

The transaction above will look something like this: repay Ann Bob 5.00

Note that money won't always be easily or equally divisible. For example, if three people have a shared bill of 12.50, that would be 4.16 each and 0.02 in the remainder. You should spread the difference between the first N persons in the list ordered by name; each person pays an extra 0.01, which is the minimum amount of money in real life. As a result, the sum will be split as follows: 4.17, 4.17, 4.16. To make it work, choose an appropriate RoundingMode for BigDecimal division.

Objectives

In this stage, your program should be able to:

  1. Process additional commands:
    group [create|show] GROUPNAME [(list of names)]
    [date] purchase Person itemName amount (GROUP)
  2. Process the input amount as money and correctly handle division;
    read decimal input as money for any command (for example, 4.99);
    output the sum with two decimal places (for example, 12.00).
  3. Calculate and display the list of repayments with names and amounts to be repaid.
Examples

The greater-than symbol followed by a space (> ) represents the user input.
The program should print a list of commands: 

> help
balance
borrow
exit
group
help
purchase
repay

Group command:

> group create lowerCaseText
Illegal command arguments
> group show NOTCREATEDGROUP
Unknown group
> group create BOYS (Elon, Bob, Chuck)
> group show BOYS
Bob
Chuck
Elon

Simple splitting:

> group create COFFEETEAM (Ann, Bob)
> purchase Bob coffee 10 (COFFEETEAM)
> balance close
Ann owes Bob 5.00
> repay Ann Bob 5.00
> balance close
No repayments

Splitting the bill and spreading the division remainder:

> group create FRIENDS (Ann, Bob, Chuck)
> purchase Elon chocolate 12.50 (FRIENDS)
> balance close
Ann owes Elon 4.17
Bob owes Elon 4.17
Chuck owes Elon 4.16

Group purchases:

> group create BOYS (Elon, Bob, Chuck)
> group create GIRLS (Ann, Diana)
> 2020.10.20 purchase Diana flowers 15.65 (BOYS)
> 2020.10.21 purchase Chuck pizza 6.30 (BOYS)
> 2020.10.22 purchase Bob icecream 3.99 (GIRLS)
> balance close
Ann owes Bob 2.00
Bob owes Chuck 2.10
Bob owes Diana 3.23
Chuck owes Diana 5.22
Elon owes Chuck 2.10
Elon owes Diana 5.21
